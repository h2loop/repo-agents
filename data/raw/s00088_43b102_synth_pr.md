## Title: Fix MCS selection to respect channel quality and UE capability

### Summary
The uplink MCS selection in `ulsch_modulation()` was not considering channel quality indicators (CQI) or UE capability constraints, leading to potentially incorrect modulation scheme selection. The original implementation directly used the configured MCS without validation, which could result in choosing overly aggressive modulation orders for poor channel conditions or exceeding UE hardware limitations.

This fix introduces adaptive MCS adjustment based on channel quality and UE capabilities before modulation mapping. The validation ensures the selected MCS aligns with actual radio conditions and device constraints, preventing unnecessary transmission failures.

### Changes
- `openair1/PHY/LTE_UE_TRANSPORT/ulsch_modulation.c`: Added MCS validation logic that:
  - Limits MCS to ≤10 (QPSK) when CQI < 7 indicates poor channel quality
  - Limits MCS to ≤20 (16QAM) when CQI < 10 indicates moderate conditions
  - Constrains MCS by `ue_cap_max_mcs` when UE capability is specified
  - Emits warning logs when MCS adjustments are applied
  - Uses the validated MCS value for subsequent modulation order calculation

### Implementation Details
The validation occurs immediately before the `get_Qm_ul()` call, replacing the direct MCS access with a validated local variable. The implementation includes a simplified CQI-based approach that would be enhanced with actual CQI reporting in production deployments.

### Testing
- Verified MCS clamping behavior at CQI boundary conditions
- Confirmed warning log generation when constraints are applied
- Validated no impact on modulation output when constraints are not triggered
